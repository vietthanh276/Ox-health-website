Lycopene

is an antioxidant that gives fruits and vegetables bright red or orange colors. It's benefits have been studied for cardiovascular health, certain cancers, prostate health, and some mental disorders. Gac Fruit has 70 times more lycopene than common fruits and vegetables. Unfortunately about 80% of lycopene consumed in the United States comes from artifical sources.

Beta-carotene

is a precursor of retinol (vitamin A) that can only be obtained from fruits and vegetables. Retinol is an important nutrient for vision and plays an important role in cell growth and maintaining healthy organs like the heart, lungs, and kidney. Gac fruit has the highest concentration of beta-carotene of any known fruit.

Docosahexaenoic acid

or DHA, is an omega-3 fatty acid that is normally found in fish and nuts. It plays an important role in the development of eye and nerve tissues, and it has been used to support cardiovascular health. DHA has also been shown to contribute to improved memory function in older adults.
